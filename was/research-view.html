Abstract

The use of virtual machines (VMs) in Cloud computing provides various benefits in the overall software engineering lifecycle. These include efficient elasticity mechanisms resulting in higher resource utilization and lower operational costs. The VMs as software artifacts are created using provider‐specific templates, called virtual machine images (VMI), and are stored in proprietary or public repositories for further use. However, some technology‐specific choices can limit the interoperability among various Cloud providers and bundle the VMIs with nonessential or redundant software packages, leading to increased storage size, prolonged VMI delivery, stagnant VMI instantiation, and ultimately vendor lock‐in.

With the right testing and research, I do not doubt we can come up with an optimised way of rendering all kinds of content types. Whether we use 2 templates for 10 types of content or distinct templates for each type.

Assuming an objective is to increase the depth of engagement across a wider range of content; our goal should be to provide a rich, useful and accessible content experience whilst also providing clear and appropriate avenues to other experiences within our content ecosystem.

Other goals should be to fulfil the needs of minority (but equally valuable) personas, including Authors, Editors, Advertisers, etc.

Features

  • Better defined publication header with key links
  • Related content section - "News" and "Research"
  • Related content area at the end of the content

Risks

  • Related content will present a problem and will become exponentially more difficult the more content types and subjects we want to support
    • Related content is just about closely matching 'concepts' and showing the results
    • Different fields of research have different life cycles for their research. Cutting edge technology research can expire more quickly than anthropology research. Shown related content should respect this.
    • Similarly, different types of content have different lifespans. Research articles can be valid for years, news stories might only be meaningful for months. Events/Webinars only occur for fixed periods of time. How do we avoid showing out-dated content even if the concept-match is identical to the content being currently viewed.
    • Do we enable related content curation? Explicitly assigning content to the related feeds based on taxonomy or our own enrichment process?
    • Do we assign related 'expiry dates' to content so that once that date passes it will no longer be brought forward into the user's related content feed.
  • Free-form related content area at the bottom of the article could interfere with comments section (if present)
